## **TASK 1 : Digital Literacy Infographic**

I created a one-page visual guide titled Navigating the Digital Frontier to help my batchmates get a handle on digital literacy. Since we’re constantly online, I wanted to show that being digitally literate isn't just about using a computer, it's about being smart, safe, and professional in how we handle information. 

### **Tool & Design**

I used Canva to build this. I'm a fan of its drag-and-drop interface, which makes it easy to organize a lot of text into clean, colorcoded units that don't feel overwhelming to read. My design breaks down four main pillars: Digital Fluency, Academic Productivity Tools, Personal Safety (Cryptography/Privacy), and Professional Correspondence.

### **Reflection**

The most interesting part of this process was the "less is more" challenge. I realized how hard it is to explain complex topics like end-to-end encryption or information synthesis in just a few bullet points. Stripping away the jargon to make the advice actually "human" and useful for a busy student was a great exercise in communication.
